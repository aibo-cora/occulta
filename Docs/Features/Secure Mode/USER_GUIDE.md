# Secure Mode User Guide

## What is Secure Mode?

Secure Mode is a powerful privacy protection system built into Occulta designed for people in sensitive situations—journalists, lawyers, human rights workers, military personnel, and anyone else who needs to protect specific contacts from coercion or forced access.

**The core idea:** If someone forces you to unlock your phone, you can use a special PIN to show a "decoy" contact list while keeping your real, sensitive contacts completely hidden.

## How Secure Mode Works

### The Two-PIN System

Secure Mode adds a second password layer to your app:

**Your Master PIN (The Real One)**
- This is your normal PIN that unlocks the full app
- You see all your real contacts and information
- Only you should know this PIN

**Your Duress PIN (The Decoy PIN)**
- A separate PIN you set up when you activate Secure Mode
- If someone forces you to unlock the app, you enter this PIN instead
- The app shows a carefully curated "fake" contact list
- Your real sensitive contacts stay hidden and encrypted

### What Happens When You Activate Secure Mode

When you turn on Secure Mode, Occulta:

1. **Asks you to choose your sensitive contacts** — these are the people you want to keep hidden
   - You select which contacts should be protected
   - These contacts disappear completely when the duress PIN is entered

2. **Creates a decoy contact list** — the phone will show this if the duress PIN is used
   - Add believable placeholder contacts
   - Create a list that looks normal to someone forcing access
   - The decoy list can include people you actually know but aren't sensitive

3. **Encrypts everything securely** — your phone's built-in security features protect the data
   - Real contacts are encrypted and locked away
   - Only accessible with your master PIN
   - The phone's secure storage keeps the encryption keys safe

4. **Sets your duress PIN** — the emergency access password
   - Different from your normal PIN
   - Never written down anywhere
   - Only you know it

## Using Secure Mode in Practice

### Normal Use (Master PIN)

When you enter your **master PIN**:
- You see all your contacts (real and decoy)
- Everything works normally
- You can send and receive messages from anyone
- All your data is visible to you

### Coercion Scenario (Duress PIN)

If someone forces you to unlock the phone:

1. **Enter your duress PIN** instead of your master PIN
2. **The app shows only the decoy contact list**
   - Sensitive contacts are completely invisible
   - The person forcing access sees a normal-looking app
   - They cannot find the people you're protecting

3. **Your real contacts remain encrypted** on the phone
   - Even law enforcement cannot decrypt them without your master PIN
   - The encryption is backed by your phone's secure hardware
   - They're protected until you unlock with your real PIN

### What the Coercer Sees

- A contact list that looks like any normal app
- No indication that Secure Mode is active
- No button to disable it (it's hidden)
- No visual difference from a regular phone
- No way to discover your real sensitive contacts

## Advanced: Multi-Layer Protection

Occulta supports **multiple duress layers** — an extra level of protection against sophisticated attackers.

### How Multi-Layer Works

Think of it like Russian nesting dolls:

- **Depth 0** (Center): Your real contacts and data
- **Depth 1** (First decoy): A convincing fake list
- **Depth 2** (Second decoy): Another fake list that looks different from Depth 1
- **Depth 3+** (More layers): Additional decoys if needed

### When You Might Use Multi-Layer

**Example Scenario:**
- You're a journalist with sensitive sources (Depth 0)
- You have an informant who knows you have a source network (Depth 1)
- That informant might be interrogated themselves (so you create Depth 2)
- Depth 2 is a completely different set of fake contacts designed to convince someone who knows what Depth 1 looks like

### How It Works

1. When you enter your **normal master PIN**: You see everything (Depth 0)
2. When you enter your **first duress PIN**: You see the first decoy (Depth 1)
3. When you enter a **second duress PIN**: You see a different decoy (Depth 2)
4. If the attacker has multiple people and keeps interrogating them, you can add more layers

## Important Security Principles

### Your PINs Are Your Only Protection

- **Never share your master PIN** with anyone (except people you fully trust with everything)
- **Your duress PIN is between you and yourself** — don't tell anyone what it is
- **Write them down only in a truly secure place** (if at all)
- **Memorize them if possible** — the safest place for a PIN is your memory

### The Encryption Is Real

- Your contacts are encrypted using military-grade encryption
- The phone's secure hardware (Secure Enclave) protects the encryption keys
- Even Apple cannot decrypt your data without your PIN
- The encryption is as strong as the passwords used by governments and military worldwide

### Your Phone Must Stay Physical Safe

- Secure Mode protects the **data** but not the **phone**
- Someone can still steal your phone, damage it, or use other methods
- Secure Mode is protection from **coerced access** (forced unlocking), not from theft

### Secure Mode Activation Takes Time

When you set up Secure Mode:
- Your phone will re-encrypt all your contact data
- This process is secure but takes a few moments
- Don't interrupt it (no force-closing the app)
- When it's done, a confirmation appears

## Setup Guide: Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Decide On Your Sensitive Contacts

Before you activate, know which contacts need protection:
- Write them down on paper (and destroy the list after)
- Or mentally note them
- Be realistic about who truly needs hidden protection

### Step 2: Design Your Decoy Contacts

Plan a fake contact list that will be convincing:
- Include people you actually know (but aren't sensitive)
- Add names of common contacts someone might expect you to have
- Make it look like a normal, small-to-medium contact list
- Don't leave it completely empty

### Step 3: Activate Secure Mode

In the app:
1. Go to Settings → Security
2. Tap "Set Up Protection"
3. Follow the prompts to:
   - Classify your contacts (sensitive vs. visible)
   - Configure your decoy contact list
   - Create your duress PIN
4. Review the summary
5. Tap "Activate"
6. Wait for the activation to complete

### Step 4: Test It (Carefully)

After activation:
1. **Enter your master PIN** — see all contacts (real + decoy)
2. **Lock and return to the app**
3. **Enter your duress PIN** — see only decoy contacts
4. **Lock and return again**
5. **Enter your master PIN** — confirm all contacts are back

**Important:** Test in a safe place, not under actual coercion.

## Common Questions

### "Can Apple/Government Access My Data?"

No. Your data is encrypted on your phone. No one can read it without:
1. Your master PIN (which you never share), AND
2. Access to your phone's secure hardware

Even if they have the phone and know the duress PIN, they only see the decoy list.

### "What If I Forget My Master PIN?"

Unfortunately, the encryption is designed so that **nobody** can decrypt your data without the correct PIN—not even Occulta developers. If you forget:
- Your sensitive contacts are permanently inaccessible
- The phone will need to be erased
- Only keep Secure Mode active if you can reliably remember your PIN

This is a feature, not a bug: it ensures your data is truly safe.

### "What If Someone Watches Me Enter the PIN?"

This is a real risk. If someone sees you:
- Enter the duress PIN, they'll believe the decoy list is real ✓
- Enter the master PIN, they know more is hidden ✗

**Recommendations:**
- Practice entering your duress PIN until it's automatic
- Block your screen with your hand or position the phone away from view
- If possible, enter the PIN when no one is watching

### "Can the App Tell Me When It's in Duress Mode?"

No. Occulta is designed to look **identical** whether you entered the master PIN or the duress PIN. There's no visible indicator. This is intentional — if an attacker sees "Secure Mode active," they know you're hiding something.

### "What About My Vault?"

Your vault (secure storage) is also protected by Secure Mode:
- With your master PIN: You see all vault items
- With your duress PIN: Vault items are hidden
- Same protection as your contacts

### "How Many Layers Can I Add?"

Technically, unlimited. Practically:
- 1 layer: You + 1 attacker who might not know about the feature
- 2 layers: Protects against an attacker who knows about one duress layer
- 3+ layers: For very sophisticated threat models

Each layer takes up secure storage on your phone (~32 KB per layer).

### "If I Add a Layer While in Duress Mode, Will They Know?"

Yes, if they're watching. Adding a layer requires:
- Navigating to Settings
- Tapping "Add Layer" 
- Following the setup process

Someone observing would see these actions. However, they can't see *what* you're configuring, only that you're in the Settings app.

### "Can I Change My PINs?"

- **Master PIN:** Change it anytime (like your app PIN)
- **Duress PIN:** Can be changed, but the process is security-sensitive
- **When in duress mode:** You can only change the duress PIN for your current layer

Changing a PIN requires re-entering your sensitive contact data, so plan ahead.

### "What Happens If Activation Fails?"

If Secure Mode activation is interrupted or fails:
- Your data remains unchanged
- No encryption has happened
- You can try again
- Contact support if errors persist

### "Can I Deactivate Secure Mode?"

Yes, but it's a deliberate process:
1. Go to Settings
2. Tap "Deactivate Protection"
3. Enter your master PIN
4. Confirm you want to deactivate
5. Your real contacts re-appear in the main contact list
6. The decoy contacts are removed

This process also takes a few moments while the phone re-encrypts everything.

## Security Reminders

✓ **Do:**
- Memorize both your master and duress PINs if possible
- Use PINs that are hard to guess (8+ characters, mix of letters/numbers)
- Test your setup in a safe place before relying on it
- Keep your phone physically secure
- Consider adding multiple layers if your threat model requires it

✗ **Don't:**
- Share your master PIN with anyone
- Write your PINs on your phone or in your notes app
- Use the same PIN for multiple purposes
- Assume Secure Mode protects a phone that's been stolen
- Activate Secure Mode under pressure (setup requires focus)

## What Secure Mode Does NOT Do

- **Does not hide that your phone is running Occulta** — the app icon is visible
- **Does not protect an unlocked phone from physical theft** — but it does protect against forced access
- **Does not prevent someone from taking your phone** — it only protects the data inside
- **Does not work if your biometric (Face ID) is forced** — use PIN-only mode if forced biometric access is a concern
- **Does not replace other security practices** — use alongside VPNs, encrypted messaging, and operational security
